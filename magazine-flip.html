Design in the
Age of Machines
When the algorithm becomes the author, what remains of intention?

For a century, design discourse revolved around the tension between function and beauty. Now a third term disrupts the dialectic: generation. Outputs that no human hand sketched, emerging from latent spaces trained on everything we have ever made.

The question is not whether these forms are beautiful — many are. The question is whether beauty without interiority can carry meaning. Design has always been a conversation; the machine is a fluent speaker who has read every book and felt nothing.

The Fold · Spring 2026
The Architecture
of Memory
Our built environments are not merely shelter — they are mnemonics cast in stone, shaping the texture of recollection.

Every corridor we walk becomes a groove in the record of self. The Proustian madeleine is well-known, yet we overlook architecture's quieter role — the particular slant of afternoon light through a transept window, the echo of footsteps in an empty atrium.

Cognitive scientists now confirm what poets long suspected: spatial navigation and episodic memory share overlapping neural substrates in the hippocampus.
